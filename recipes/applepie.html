Ingredients

      Granny Smith apples
      Flour
      Sugar
      Milk
      Cinnamon
      Butter
      Salt

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Start by making the pie crust. You can either use a store-bought crust or make your own. If making your own crust, combine 2 ½ cups of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1 tablespoon of sugar in a large bowl. Cut 1 cup of cold butter into small pieces and add it to the flour mixture. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add 6-8 tablespoons of cold milk while stirring until the dough comes together. Divide the dough in half, shape into disks, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. While the dough is chilling, prepare the filling. Peel, core, and slice about 6 Granny Smith apples into thin slices. In a separate bowl, combine 3/4 cup of sugar, 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of cinnamon, and a pinch of salt. Mix well.
  4. Roll out one of the chilled dough disks on a floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Transfer the rolled-out dough to the pie dish, gently pressing it into the bottom and up the sides.
  5. Place the sliced apples into the prepared pie crust. Sprinkle the sugar, flour, cinnamon, and salt mixture over the apples. Dot the top with small pieces of butter (about 2 tablespoons).
  6. Roll out the second chilled dough disk and place it over the apple filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges of the crust to seal the pie.
  7. Use a sharp knife to cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.
  8. Optional: Brush the top crust with a little milk and sprinkle with sugar for a golden and slightly sweet finish.
  9. Place the pie on a baking sheet (to catch any drips) and bake in the preheated oven for 45 to 55 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  10. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before serving. This will help the filling set.
